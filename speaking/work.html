Speaking Part 1: Work

What is your job?

I work as a chef. I've been doing this for about a year. I started before I moved to Canada. I found this job through my sister's friend. It's a very busy job.

Where do you work?

I work at a restaurant in Saskatchewan. It's a small restaurant. I usually go there by car, and sometimes by bus. It takes me 20 minutes to get there. The place is quite warm and clean.

Why did you choose that job?

I chose this job because I love cooking. I want to make a lot of new dishes. It suits my personality. In fact, it's not good for me because my skin is very thin so I'm allergic to heat.

Is it a popular job in your country?

Yes, it's a very popular job in my country. Many people do this job, especially young men. In my cooking class, most of the students are men. It's a common job because there are a lot of restaurants and food stalls in my country. In cities, you see a lot of people doing this job, but in rural areas, it's less common.

Do you like your job?

Yes, I like my job because I meet a lot of people. I enjoy talking to customers. However, some parts are difficult, like lifting heavy pans. I feel happy when I see my customers happy. It's not perfect, but it's not stressful.

Do you get on well with your colleagues?

Yes, I get along well with my coworkers. They are very lovely and supportive. We help each other with preparing food. We sometimes have lunch together. It feels like a small family.

What was your first day like?

On my first day, I felt quite shy and nervous because everything was new and I didn't know anybody there. But everybody was friendly to me. I had to learn how to do my job. At the end of the day, I felt quite exhausted.

What responsibilities do you have at work?

At work, I'm responsible for restocking, preparing and cooking food. I also help with putting the food on the dish. I start work at 9 am and finish at 4 pm.

If you had the chance, would you change your job?

If I had the chance, I would still do my job because I love my job. It makes me happy. Moreover, changing jobs can be risky. I may have to go far from my house.

Do you plan to continue with your job in the future?

In the future, I want to open my own restaurant. If possible, I will learn to teach cooking and open a cooking school.
